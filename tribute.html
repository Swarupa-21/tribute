Mahatma Gandhi

The man who led India's freedom struggle through Truth and Non-violence

Revered the world over for his nonviolent philosophy of passive resistance, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was known to his many followers as Mahatma, or “the great-souled one.” His life and teachings inspired activists including Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.

Timeline:

  • October 2, 1869: Birth of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi
  • 1883: Gandhi and Kasturbai are married.
  • September 4, 1888: ·Gandhi leaves for England to study law.
  • April 1893: ·Gandhi accepts commission to spend a year in South Africa advising on a lawsuit.
  • 1901: Gandhi returns to India to attend the Indian National Congress.
  • May 25, 1915: Gandhi and his followers found Satyagraha ashram, the religiously-oriented communal farm where Gandhi, his family, and his followers will live.
  • March 12-April 6, 1931: Gandhi leads his Salt March to the sea.
  • Aug 8 1942: The All India Congress Committee Launches the Quit India Movement.
  • Jun 15 1947: United Kingdom Passes the Indian Independence Act 1947
  • Aug 15 1947: India gets Independence.
  • Jan 30 1948 Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is Assassinated by Nathuram Godse
